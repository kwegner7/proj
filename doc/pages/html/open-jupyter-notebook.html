How To Open a CSV File in a Jupyter Notebook


It is required that Anaconda Navigator be installed on your PC.

In the search window at lower left, type "anaconda"
Select "Anaconda Powershell Prompt"

Specify the location of the "apps" folder:

$APPS = "C:\res\code\apps"

There is a "jupyter" folder containing a "data" folder. This is a useful place to store the CSV files.

Type the following commands:

set-location $APPS/jupyter
jupyter notebook AirDepth.ipynb
Pull down "Kernel" and select Restart & Run All"

These are the meanings of each column:

x1, y1, z1
The 3D Cartesian coordinates of the burst with origin on the surface of the earth. The x coordinate is the horizontal, the y coordinate is the depth and the z coordinate is the vertical component. Units are feet.
x2, y2, z2
The 3D Cartesian coordinates of the receiver with origin on the surface of the earth. The x coordinate is the horizontal, the y coordinate is the depth and the z coordinate is the vertical component. Units are feet.
alt1, alt2
The altitudes of the burst and receiver. The altitude is greater than or equal to the z coordinate because of the curvature of the earth. Units are feet.
horiz, vert
The horizontal and vertical offsets from the burst to the receiver. Units are feet.
slant
The slant range from the burst to the receiver. Units are feet.
angle
The angle of the burst to the receiver. Units are degrees. This angle is measured from the horizontal plane containing the burst location.
density1, density2
The air densities of the burst and receiver at alt1 and alt2. Units are grams per centimeter cubed.
rhor
The air depth (rhor) between the burst and receiver. Units are grams per centimeter squared.

Under the heading "Open a CSV File", you can specify any CSV file in the data folder:

res = pd.read_csv('data/ballistic7.csv')
